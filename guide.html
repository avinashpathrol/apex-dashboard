Understanding
Trading Signals
The bot uses 6 technical indicators to score market conditions. It needs at least 3 signals in the same direction to place a trade. Here's what each one means.
ℹ️
How scoring works: Each indicator can contribute 1 bullish or 1 bearish signal. The bot counts them up — 3+ bullish = BUY, 3+ bearish = SELL, anything else = HOLD.
RSI — Relative Strength Index
Bullish + Bearish
RSI measures momentum on a scale of 0–100. When it's very low, the asset is oversold (beaten down too much) and likely to bounce. When very high, it's overbought and likely to pull back. The bot watches RSI to catch these turning points.
BULLISH → RSI < 35 (oversold) OR RSI < 50 and rising
BEARISH → RSI > 65 (overbought) OR RSI > 50 and falling
Window: 14 periods (each period = 15 min candle)
MACD — Moving Average Convergence Divergence
Bullish + Bearish
MACD tracks the relationship between two moving averages. When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, momentum is shifting bullish. A cross below signals the opposite. It's one of the most reliable trend-change indicators.
BULLISH → MACD crosses above signal line (crossover)
BULLISH → MACD already above signal + histogram positive
BEARISH → MACD crosses below signal line (crossover)
BEARISH → MACD below signal + histogram negative
Settings: 12 / 26 / 9 (standard)
EMA-20 — 20-Period Exponential Moving Average
Bullish + Bearish
The EMA-20 is the average price of the last 20 candles, weighted to give more importance to recent prices. If the current price is above it, the short-term trend is up. Below it means short-term downtrend.
BULLISH → Current price > EMA-20 (price above short-term average)
BEARISH → Current price < EMA-20 (price below short-term average)
Period: 20 candles × 15 min = 5 hours
EMA-50 — 50-Period Exponential Moving Average
Bullish + Bearish
The EMA-50 captures the medium-term trend. The relationship between EMA-20 and EMA-50 is classic "golden cross / death cross" logic. When the faster average is above the slower one, it indicates sustained upward momentum.
BULLISH → EMA-20 > EMA-50 (short-term above medium-term = uptrend)
BEARISH → EMA-20 < EMA-50 (short-term below medium-term = downtrend)
Period: 50 candles × 15 min = ~12.5 hours
Bollinger Bands — Volatility Envelope
Bullish + Bearish
Bollinger Bands draw an upper and lower boundary around price based on volatility. When price touches the lower band, it's statistically stretched too far down and often bounces. Upper band touch suggests the opposite.
BULLISH → Price in bottom 20% of BB range (near lower band)
BEARISH → Price in top 20% of BB range (near upper band)
Settings: 20 period, 2 standard deviations
Volume Confirmation
Confirming only
Volume doesn't trigger trades on its own — it confirms them. A big price move on high volume is much more reliable than the same move on low volume. The bot uses this as a tiebreaker to strengthen the dominant signal direction.
Triggers when: current volume > 1.3× average of last 10 candles
BULLISH → High volume when more bullish signals active
BEARISH → High volume when more bearish signals active
Step by Step
How Each Trade is Taken
Every 7–15 minutes the bot wakes up, runs through this exact process, and makes a decision. Here's the full lifecycle of a trade.
🔄
The Bot Cycle
Runs every ~7–15 min via two GitHub Actions workflows (offset by 7 min to reduce gaps)
1
Fetch Market Data
Calls Kraken API to get the last 100 OHLC candles (15-minute intervals) for BTC/CAD. This gives ~25 hours of price history — enough for all indicators to be accurate.
2
Calculate All 6 Indicators
RSI, MACD, EMA-20, EMA-50, Bollinger Bands and Volume are all calculated fresh from the latest data. No cached values — everything is recalculated every single run.
3
Score Signals
Each indicator votes bullish or bearish (or neither). Votes are tallied. If bullish ≥ 3 AND bullish > bearish → BUY. If bearish ≥ 3 AND bearish > bullish → SELL. Otherwise → HOLD.
4
Check Balance
Before executing, the bot checks your Kraken account. For BUY: needs ≥ $20 CAD available. For SELL: needs actual BTC balance. If either is missing, the trade is SKIPPED (not an error — this is intentional protection).
5
Execute Market Order
Places a market order on Kraken for $20 CAD worth of BTC. Market orders fill instantly at the current best price. The transaction ID is logged for your records.
6
Send Telegram Alert
Instantly sends you a Telegram message with the trade details — price, quantity, confidence, signals, and estimated P&L on sells.
7
Log Summary
Writes a JSON summary to GitHub Actions logs. The dashboard reads these logs to populate trade history, signal displays, and all statistics.
⚡
Confidence Score
How certain the bot is about each trade
The confidence score (shown as a % in the history table) is calculated as:

confidence = 55 + (dominant_signals / total_signals) × 45

So a trade with 3 signals where all 3 are bullish gets ~85%. A trade with 4 bullish and 1 bearish gets a bit less because there's some disagreement. Maximum is capped at 95% — the bot never claims to be 100% certain.

Risk level is set to LOW if 4+ signals align, MEDIUM if exactly 3.
Protecting Your Capital
Risk Management
The bot has built-in risk rules to protect your account. Here's every limit and why it exists.
⚠️
Important: The bot uses signal-based exits (SELL when bearish signals ≥ 3), not hard stop-loss orders on Kraken. The TP/SL lines on the In Trade chart are reference levels to help you visualise risk — not automatic triggers.
ParameterValueWhy
Trade size $20 CAD Small fixed amount to prove the strategy before scaling. Limits max loss per trade to a few cents.
Min signals to trade 3 of 6 Reduces false signals. A single indicator being wrong won't trigger a trade.
Take Profit target +3.5% Reference level shown on In Trade chart. Bot exits on signal, not this number.
Stop Loss reference -2.0% Reference level shown on In Trade chart. If price falls 2% from entry, a SELL signal usually follows naturally.
One trade at a time Yes Bot checks balance before every trade. Won't double-buy or sell what it doesn't have.
HIGH risk trades Skipped If risk is flagged HIGH (future feature), the trade is skipped regardless of signals.
Market order type Market Fills instantly at best available price. No slippage risk from limit orders not filling.
Kraken trading fee ~0.26% Maker/taker fee on Kraken. Tracked in the fees section of dashboard.
📉
Worst Case on $20 CAD
Maximum realistic loss per trade
If BTC drops -2% from your entry before the bot signals a SELL:

$20.00 × -2% = -$0.40 CAD loss

Plus Kraken fees for both BUY and SELL:
$20.00 × 0.26% × 2 = ~$0.10 in fees

Total worst case: ~$0.50 CAD — less than a coffee. This is why starting small to prove the strategy makes sense before scaling to $150–200 CAD.
📈
Best Case on $20 CAD
At the +3.5% take profit level
If BTC rises +3.5% and the bot catches the SELL signal near the top:

$20.00 × 3.5% = +$0.70 CAD profit

Minus fees:
-$0.10 in fees

Net: ~$0.60 CAD — small now, but at $200 CAD that same move = +$6.00 per trade. Multiple trades per day compounds quickly.

Quick Reference
Glossary
Plain-English definitions for every term you'll see on the dashboard.
BUY / SELL / HOLD
BUY — Bot placed an order to purchase BTC. SELL — Bot sold all BTC. HOLD — Not enough signals to act. Waiting for clearer conditions.
SKIPPED
A trade was signalled but couldn't execute. Either no CAD to buy with, or no BTC to sell. Click the SKIPPED badge in History for the specific reason.
Unrealised P&L
Profit or loss on your current open position if you sold right now. It's "on paper" — becomes real when the bot sells.
Realised P&L
Profit or loss that has been locked in after a SELL. This is real money that moved in your account.
Confidence %
How strongly the signals agree. 88% means 88% of the signal scoring weight points in the trade direction. Higher = stronger conviction.
OHLC
Open, High, Low, Close — the four price points for each 15-minute candle. The bot uses the closing price for most calculations.
Take Profit (TP)
A target price where a winning trade could be closed. Set at +3.5% above entry on the In Trade chart. The bot exits on signals, not this fixed level.
Stop Loss (SL)
A reference price showing the −2% level below entry. If price hits here, bearish signals usually follow and the bot will SELL. Not a hard stop order on Kraken.
EMA (Exponential Moving Average)
An average price over N candles, weighted to give more importance to recent prices. Faster to react than a simple moving average.
Bollinger Bands
Two lines drawn 2 standard deviations above and below a 20-period moving average. They expand when volatility is high and contract when it's low.
Market Order
An order that executes immediately at the best available current price. Used by the bot for instant fills — no waiting for a specific price.
Win Rate
Percentage of completed trades that were profitable. 60% win rate means 6 out of every 10 closed trades made money.
